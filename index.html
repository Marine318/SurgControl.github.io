Abstract

TL;DR:SurgControl is a unified framework for generating high-fidelity and controllable endoscopic videos conditioned on a single reference image and structural guidance, featuring a Hybrid-Guided Video Diffusion Architecture that outperforms state-of-the-art benchmarks in visual quality and controllability.

Synthesizing realistic endoscopic surgical videos holds significant promise for medical education, surgical training, and data augmentation for AI algorithms. However, existing approaches often suffer from the inability to stably generate high-fidelity videos and lack precise controllability over surgical motions.

To address these challenges, we propose SurgControl, a unified framework for generating high-fidelity and controllable endoscopic videos conditioned on a single reference image and structural guidance. Specifically, SurgControl first introduce a data curation pipeline integrating Multimodal Large Language Models and annotation tools. Subsequently, SurgControl establish a large-scale multi-modal endoscopic dataset (Video-Text-Structure), effectively mitigating the issues of data scarcity and high annotation costs in this domain. Building upon this dataset, we present a Hybrid-Guided Video Diffusion Architecture. To achieve fine-grained control over surgical instruments, we leverage a pre-trained Large Video Diffusion Model as the generative backbone and introduce a light-weight Structural Control Module to encode structure sequences into dynamic guidance features.

Experiments demonstrate that SurgControl outperforms state-of-the-art benchmarks in visual quality and controllability, establishing a new paradigm for medical video synthesis.
